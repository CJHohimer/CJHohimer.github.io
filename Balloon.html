Weather Balloon Launching

While attending Walla Walla Community College I helped design and launch several weather balloons, reaching a maximum altitude of ~95,000 ft. We used a 1200 gram balloon from scientificsales.com. Based on the mass of the camera pod we filled the balloon with approximately 200 ft3. We used APRS as the primary means of tracking the balloon during flight. It would transmit data (altitude, speed, direction, location) every 30 seconds. The chase & recovery vehicle carried a radio receiver and laptop to map the transmitted coordinates and recover the pod after landing. On the 2nd launch in 2011 this worked so well we captured a final photo from the chase car while the parachute was still partially inflated.
