Shoulder Support Exo-Suit

As a postdoctoral fellow I have been working on the design and testing of a shoulder support Exo-Suit for industrial workers. The system is designed around a pneumatic actuator integrated into a shirt with a backpack pneumatic supply. The goal is to reduce muscle activity of industrial workers performing overhead activities. I have been leading the design and development effort and working to test the device in our motion capture lab to show efficacy.

Development for our penumatic supply started with on a benchtop testbed I build. It runs on a beaglebone running Debian OS. The control software has been developed by myself and a Ph.D. student in the Bio-design lab.

The testbed was used to understand pump performance and low level controller development. It also allowed me to learn the beaglebone and developing c++ code on that platform.

We then packaged the hardware into a packpack like system to be worn with out suit and actuators.

The system has a user handle to control the pressure input as well as toggle the arm support on and off.
