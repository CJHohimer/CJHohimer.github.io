Curriculum Vitae

I am currently a Research Associate in the Biodesign Lab under the direction of Conor Walsh. My research interests include soft-robotics, wearables, exosuits, machine design. With my own lab, I hope to bridge the game between material science and manufacturing, and wearable robotics technology. I want to explore new ways to create functional robots for unstructured environments and wearable technology to interact with these robots to enhance human performance. On this site, you will find a variety of research and personal projects that I have worked on and found interesting. Most importantly, things here are a work in progress and everything needs updating

When I am not in the lab I enjoy as much of the great outdoors as I can. I enjoy hiking, camping, all types of boating. I also enjoy riding my bike for both commuting and weekend touring. Sport lockpicking is also a hobby I recently picked up during the 2020 shutdown. I enjoying practicing my picking skills while watching Netflix with my wife, Katie. I am no locksmith (yet), but I can open many of the padlocks and simple cylinders in my collection.

Prior to my academic career I spent six years in the US Navy. In that I made two deployments to the middle east as a combat coxswain/radio operator, and a joint terminal attack controller. I enjoyed the work but decided there were other things in life I wanted to pursue. I was honorably discharged in September of 2009.

I also love all things train-related. My grandfather was a huge train enthusiast and past along both his love of mechanical engineering as well as model trains. As I kid I would enjoy my summers riding his large scale garden train around his property on the track around his house. I look forward to the day I can emulate this at my own house and restore my steam locomotive to its glory days.
